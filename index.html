Save The Sea | App

Save The Sea

It is time to change the 'out of sight, out of mind' approach to problems across the globe's oceans. Save The Sea is an app designed to entertain and educate users on sea pollution. A fellow student and I coded and designed the game using Photoshop and Unity.

We designed the game to have a vibrant blue house style to reflect that of the sea. Children are highly engaged with vibrant colours and by imaginative and unique characters. We resulted in two characters - the scuba diver and the crab.

GNT | Design and Motion

GNT

GNT is an integrated project which aims is to educate users on all aspects of gin. This is done through interactivity on the website and a visually stimulating Motion Graphic. A forum is also included where users who sign up to GNT can interact, ask questions and share knowledge together. I designed the cohesive brand identity for GNT and took the photographs used on the website.

These are some assets used on the GNT website.

The process of how gin is created is a very interesting procedure so it was decided that a Motion Graphic would be created to feature on the website. Creating a Motion Graphics video allowed for a large amount of impact and information to be gained in a short time. Also having different types of media platforms would make the website more enticing. The key applications to produce this video were After Effects and Photoshop.

Gallo | Motion Graphic

Gallo

To celebrate summer, Gallo wanted to produce some fun and colourful posts advertising their ‘Spritz’ collection. The flavours presented were Raspberry and Lime, Peach and Nectarine, Wild Strawberry, and Pineapple and Passionfruit.

I was given a lot of freedom with this brief and could decide the movement, positioning, timing, assets and music used in each video.

YMCA | 360 Video

YMCA

During my university placement year at Southpaw, West Kent YMCA launched a campaign called ‘Don’t Look The Other Way’. Southpaw set out to create a 360-degree video that would highlight how the general public would look away when encountering homeless people on the street.

I played a big role in this project. I was given the responsibility of filming and editing. I had to ensure that what we wanted to create was achievable with our technology and that ideas put forward for the video were brought to life to the best of our best ability. I also directed Becky, a girl whose life was turned around because of the YMCA, during the shoot.

The film was released on Facebook, YouTube, Instagram and Twitter, with paid media to boost the posts. Uniquely designed Google Cardboards were distributed to members of the public for donations in Tunbridge Wells shopping centre. A stand was set up where they could experience the film using an Oculus Rift. The footage was gathered from the fundraising day which was then used to create the case study video. Seeing the reactions of the public was incredible and I was happy to see how our work had impacted people.

In addition to filming on the shoot, I also did the photography that was used to create posters and advertisements.

Finally, I created an internal emailer using HTML and CSS. This involved testing my code on many various browsers and emails to ensure that it was always compatible.

Miller | Motion Graphic

Miller

Miller wanted to repurpose some content so that it could be used on their social media again. I, therefore, was given the task of taking an image and turning it into a moving cinemagraph. This would add life and create a seamless looping video which can be used across their media platforms. To do this I manipulated the images given to me on Photoshop. I cut out different sections of the image and saved out multiple different layers which I could then manipulate on After Effects to create looping movement. The different movement I added included, drips, steam, opening a bottle and light flares. Although this is a relatively simple idea it was very effective and created some great content. In After Effects I utilised a plugin called Trapcode Particular to create my desired look.
